Sentiment analysis is a core task in natural language processing that aims to automatically determine the emotional tone of a text, such as whether it expresses a positive or negative opinion. More generally, it belongs to the broader area of text classification, where documents are assigned to predefined categories based on their content. Applications range from analyzing product reviews and social media posts to spam detection and author identification. 
In this assignment, we focus on an author classification task: given a tweet, the goal is to predict whether it was posted by Elon Musk or Justin Bieber. 
We use a dataset from Hugging Face containing 180 tweets per author. We will go through the full machine learning pipeline, including data cleaning, preprocessing, feature extraction, model training, and evaluation. This allows us to understand each step involved in building a text classification system from raw text to final predictions.
